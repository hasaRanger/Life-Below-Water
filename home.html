Why It Matters...

Life below water is a critical part of the Earth's ecosystem. It is home to a wide variety of marine life, and provides food and livelihoods for millions of people. However, the oceans are under threat from pollution, overfishing, and climate change. By conserving marine life, we can help protect the oceans and ensure a sustainable future for all.

Platic pollution

Plastic pollution is the accumulation of plastic in the environment, which harms wildlife, humans, and ecosystems

Overfishing

Overfishing occurs when too many fish in a particular stock are caught and there are not enough adults to breed and sustain a healthy population

Coral Reefs Conservation

Coral reefs are valuable ecosystems that provide food, tourism, and coastal protection.

Climate Change & Ocean Acidification

Ocean acidification is a result of climate change and is caused by increasing carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions.

How We Are Making a Difference

  • Restoring coral reefs
